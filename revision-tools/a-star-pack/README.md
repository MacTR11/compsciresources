# A* Stretch Pack

Targeted resources for the **A/A\* boundary** — where marks come from *application (AO2)*, *evaluation (AO3)* and **synoptic** (joined-up) understanding, not just recall.

## Contents
| Resource | What it does |
|----------|--------------|
| [1 — Annotated model answers](01-annotated-model-answers.md) | Full-mark exemplar answers with examiner "why this scores" commentary, plus a weaker response for contrast — including a 12-mark levels-of-response essay |
| [2 — Examiners' report insights](02-examiners-report-insights.md) | The recurring mistakes examiners flag, by topic, with what to do instead, and a "top 10 marks students throw away" list |
| [3 — Synoptic stretch questions](03-synoptic-stretch-questions.md) | Hard, cross-topic questions (with model answers) that reward connecting areas of the spec |
| [4 — Topic connections map](04-topic-connections-map.md) | A map of how H446 topics interlink, with "if you understand X, connect it to Y" prompts |

## How to use
- Use it **after** your recall is secure (organisers, flashcards, quizzes done).
- Practise the synoptic questions closed-book, then study the model answers for *technique*, not just content.
- Read the examiners' insights the week before each paper — it's some of the cheapest marks available.

> A\* is decided by *how* you answer (link to the scenario, compare, justify, conclude), not just *what* you know. Everything here drills that.
