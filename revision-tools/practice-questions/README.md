# Practice Questions Hub

Active recall through exam-style questions is the single most effective revision activity. Every topic file already contains **exam-style questions with full collapsible mark schemes** — this hub tells you how to use them well and points you to each set.

---

## How to practise (so it actually raises your grade)

1. **Closed-book, timed.** Give each question ~1 minute per mark. No notes.
2. **Mark yourself honestly** against the mark scheme. Award a mark only for points the scheme credits.
3. **Log every dropped mark** with the *reason* (see [common-mistakes](../common-mistakes.md) → personal error log).
4. **Re-RAG** the sub-topic on your [tracker](../self-assessment-tracker.md).
5. **Re-attempt** anything below ~70% after a few days.

> 💡 **A\* habit:** prioritise the **AO2/AO3** questions (apply, compare, evaluate, justify, trace, write-the-algorithm). AO1 recall is the easy half of the marks; the top grades are decided on application.

## Build your exam stamina with full past papers

Topic questions build knowledge; **whole past papers build exam technique** (timing, switching topics, stamina). Once your RAG tracker is mostly green:

1. Sit a **full past paper** under timed conditions (2h 30m each for 01 and 02).
2. Mark it against the **official OCR mark scheme**.
3. Read the **examiners' report** for that paper — it tells you exactly what students got wrong.
4. Feed weak areas back into your tracker and topic revision.

> Past papers and mark schemes are on the OCR website and widely mirrored (e.g. PMT, exam-paper sites). Always mark against the **official** mark scheme.

---

## A mixed-topic "interleaving" challenge

Once a week, build a 20-mark mini-set by pulling **one question from each of 5 different topics** at random. Mixing topics like this mirrors the real paper and exposes weak links that block-revision hides.
