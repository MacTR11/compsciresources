# Topic Mini-Papers — Bite-Size Mock Exams

Full past papers are essential, but they're long and mix every topic. **Mini-papers** let you exam-test **one topic at a time** in ~30–35 minutes — perfect after you've revised a topic and want to prove you can apply it under timed conditions before committing to a whole paper.

Each mini-paper is a self-contained **~25–30 mark** mock with realistic OCR-style questions (mixed tariffs and AOs) **and a full mark scheme** at the end so you can mark yourself immediately.

> These complement — they don't replace — official OCR past papers. Once your mini-papers are scoring well, move to full timed past papers marked against the official schemes.

## How to use a mini-paper (properly)

1. **Revise the topic first** (read the topic file, drill its flashcards).
2. **Closed-book and timed.** Cover the mark scheme. Give yourself the stated time — pacing is a skill you're practising too.
3. **Show working** on calculations, traces and logic — method marks are real marks.
4. **Mark honestly** against the scheme. Award a mark only for points the scheme credits.
5. **Log every dropped mark** with the reason (see [common-mistakes → error log](../common-mistakes.md)).
6. **Re-RAG** the sub-topic on your [tracker](../self-assessment-tracker.md). Below ~70%? Re-revise and re-attempt in a few days.

> 💡 **A\* habit:** for "compare/justify/evaluate/discuss" questions, check the mark scheme didn't just want *more facts* — it wanted a **judgement** or a developed, balanced argument. That gap is exactly where A\* marks live.

## Build toward the real thing

- **One topic shaky?** Do its mini-paper.
- **A component feeling solid?** Sit its mini-papers back-to-back for a longer session.
- **Exam approaching?** Move to **full official past papers**, timed, marked against OCR mark schemes, and read the **examiners' reports** — then loop weak areas back through these mini-papers and flashcards.

Targeted, timed, marked, logged — that loop is how a pass becomes an A\*.
