## Loop cards (dominoes)

A closed loop of **15 cards** mixing definitions and quick conversions. Each card states a value/term ("I have…") and asks a question ("Who has…"). The answer to every question is the "I have" on another card; the chain returns to the start.

**How to play**
1. Cut out the 15 cards and deal them so every student has at least one (some may hold two).
2. Pick any starter — read its "Who has…" question aloud.
3. The student whose card answers it reads "I have… Who has…", and play continues.
4. The loop is complete when you return to the starting card. Time the class and try to beat it.

| Card | I have… | Who has… |
|------|---------|----------|
| 1  | **0xD6** | …the denary value of binary `1010`? |
| 2  | **10** | …the result of `0 + 0` in binary? |
| 3  | **0** | …the number of bits in a nibble? |
| 4  | **4** | …the denary value of hex `F`? |
| 5  | **15** | …the denary value of binary `00100000`? |
| 6  | **32** | …the hex value of binary `1111`? |
| 7  | **F** | …the data structure that is LIFO? |
| 8  | **Stack** | …the data structure that is FIFO? |
| 9  | **Queue** | …the 8-bit two's complement of denary `−1`? |
| 10 | **11111111** | …the denary value of binary `00001000`? |
| 11 | **8** | …the effect of a logical left shift by 1, as a multiplication? |
| 12 | **Multiply by 2** | …the law that states `¬(A·B) = ¬A + ¬B`? |
| 13 | **De Morgan's** | …the floating-point part that sets the precision? |
| 14 | **Mantissa** | …the value of `0xD6` (214) minus 200, in denary? |
| 15 | **14** | …the hex value of denary `214`? |

*Loop closes:* card 15's answer (`214` in hex = **D6** → `0xD6`) leads back to **card 1**. ✓

**Verification of every conversion:** binary `1010` = 10 ✓ · `0+0` = 0 ✓ · nibble = 4 bits ✓ · hex F = 15 ✓ · binary `00100000` = 32 ✓ · binary `1111` = hex F ✓ · two's complement of −1 = `11111111` ✓ · binary `00001000` = 8 ✓ · left shift ×1 = ×2 ✓ · 214 − 200 = 14 ✓ · denary 214 = hex D6 ✓.

## Taboo cards

**How to play**
- One student describes the **TERM** at the top of the card to their team **without saying any of the 4 banned words** (or obvious word forms of them).
- 30 seconds per card, 1 point per correct guess. If a banned word slips out, the card is forfeited.
- Rotate the describer each round.

| # | TERM | Banned words |
|---|------|-------------|
| 1 | **Two's complement** | negative · MSB · invert · add one |
| 2 | **Normalisation** | mantissa · precision · point · leading |
| 3 | **Stack** | LIFO · push · pop · top |
| 4 | **De Morgan's** | bar · NOT · swap · AND/OR |
| 5 | **Hash table** | key · function · collision · index |
| 6 | **Linked list** | node · pointer · next · traverse |
| 7 | **Logical shift** | bits · multiply · divide · zero |
| 8 | **Binary search tree** | left · right · subtree · ordered |

## Bingo — binary/hex number bingo

Students fill a **3×3 grid** by choosing any 9 values from the word bank below (written as the binary or hex **pattern**, not the denary). The caller reads a **denary value or a clue**; students cross off the matching pattern. First to a line (or full house) wins.

**How to play**
1. Hand out blank 3×3 grids. Each student writes 9 different items chosen from the 20-item word bank (copy them exactly, e.g. `binary 01100100` or `hex C8`).
2. The caller works down the numbered caller list, reading the **clue** (left column) aloud — NOT the answer.
3. Students cross off a cell only if its pattern equals the called value.
4. Call "line" for a row/column/diagonal, or "full house" for all nine. Verify against the answers column.

**Word bank (20 items — write any 9 on your grid):**

`binary 00001010` · `binary 00010100` · `binary 00011110` · `binary 00101000` · `binary 01100100` · `binary 11111111` · `binary 10000000` · `binary 01000000` · `binary 00010000` · `binary 00000001` · `hex 0F` · `hex 1E` · `hex 32` · `hex 64` · `hex FF` · `hex 80` · `hex 0A` · `hex 2D` · `hex C8` · `hex 19`

**Caller list (read the clue; answer = the value students must match):**

| # | Caller reads (clue) | Value | Patterns that match |
|---|---------------------|-------|---------------------|
| 1  | "Ten"                                            | 10  | `binary 00001010`, `hex 0A` |
| 2  | "Twenty"                                          | 20  | `binary 00010100` |
| 3  | "Thirty"                                          | 30  | `binary 00011110`, `hex 1E` |
| 4  | "Forty"                                           | 40  | `binary 00101000` |
| 5  | "One hundred"                                     | 100 | `binary 01100100`, `hex 64` |
| 6  | "The largest value in one byte (unsigned)"        | 255 | `binary 11111111`, `hex FF` |
| 7  | "Two to the power 7"                              | 128 | `binary 10000000`, `hex 80` |
| 8  | "Two to the power 6"                              | 64  | `binary 01000000` |
| 9  | "Two to the power 4"                              | 16  | `binary 00010000` |
| 10 | "The smallest positive whole number"             | 1   | `binary 00000001` |

## Quick-fire "Guess the term/value"

**How to play**
- Read a clue; students write or shout the answer. Keep it fast — 10–15 seconds each.
- Mix of definitions and one-step conversions; answers verified below.

| # | Clue | Answer |
|---|------|--------|
| 1  | The signed representation where the MSB has a negative place value. | **Two's complement** |
| 2  | Convert binary `11001010` to denary. | **202** |
| 3  | Convert denary `45` to hex. | **2D** |
| 4  | The float part that determines the **range** of representable values. | **Exponent** |
| 5  | A right shift that **copies the sign bit** in from the left. | **Arithmetic (right) shift** |
| 6  | LIFO data structure with push, pop and peek. | **Stack** |
| 7  | Convert hex `BE` to denary. | **190** |
| 8  | An ordered, **immutable** sequence such as `(x, y)`. | **Tuple** |
| 9  | The half-adder output given by `A · B`. | **Carry** |
| 10 | Two keys mapping to the same index in a hash table. | **Collision** |
| 11 | Convert binary `1111` to hex. | **F** |
| 12 | An in-order traversal of a BST visits the nodes in this order. | **Ascending order** |

**Verification:** binary `11001010` = 128+64+8+2 = 202 ✓ · denary 45 = `0010 1101` = 2D ✓ · hex BE = (11×16)+14 = 190 ✓ · binary `1111` = hex F ✓ · half-adder carry = A·B ✓ · in-order of BST = ascending ✓.
