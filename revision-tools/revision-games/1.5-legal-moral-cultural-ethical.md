## Loop cards (dominoes)

A closed loop of 15 cards. Each card has an **ANSWER** (top) and the **next CLUE** (bottom). Hand them out, pick any starter, read the clue aloud; whoever holds the matching answer reads next. The chain returns to the start.

**How to play**
1. Print and cut out the 15 cards. Shuffle and deal them all.
2. One player reads the **CLUE** on the bottom of any card.
3. The player whose card **ANSWER** matches that clue reads it out, then reads *their* clue.
4. Continue until the loop closes back to the first card. If it doesn't close, a match is wrong — find it.

## Taboo cards

One player describes the **TERM** without saying any **BANNED** word (or an obvious form of it); the team guesses. 30 seconds each.

**How to play**
1. Print and cut out the 8 cards; shuffle into a pile face down.
2. The describer takes the top card and gets their team to say the TERM aloud.
3. They may **not** say the TERM or any BANNED word (or close variants). Opponents watch for slips.
4. Score 1 per correct guess in the time limit; pass if stuck.

## Scenario card sort

Sort the 10 scenario cards under the **four laws**: Data Protection Act / GDPR · Computer Misuse Act 1990 · Copyright, Designs & Patents Act 1988 · RIPA 2000.

**How to play**
1. Lay out four header cards (the four Acts). Print and cut the 10 scenarios.
2. In pairs, place each scenario under the law it breaches. For CMA cards, also call out which **offence** (1, 2 or 3).
3. Reveal the answer key. Discuss the one card where **two laws** could apply.

**Scenario cards**
- **A.** A firm sells its mailing list to advertisers without any lawful basis or consent.
- **B.** A games studio's source code is copied and released by a rival without permission.
- **C.** An employee uses a colleague's login to snoop on files but changes nothing.
- **D.** A police force intercepts a suspect's phone calls without the required authorisation.
- **E.** A user uploads pirated films to a streaming site for others to download.
- **F.** A hacker breaks into a server and deletes the company's records out of spite.
- **G.** A hospital leaves patient records on an unencrypted, publicly accessible drive.
- **H.** A criminal gains access to a network in order to plant spyware and steal banking logins.
- **I.** A company keeps job-applicant CVs indefinitely with no reason to retain them.
- **J.** Someone uses a band's song in a commercial advert without buying a licence.
