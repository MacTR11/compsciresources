# Revision Games — Active, Social Recall

Games turn revision into **retrieval practice** that doesn't feel like work — perfect for end-of-topic lessons, the run-up to mocks, revision clubs, or a change of pace at home. Every topic has a **ready-to-print games pack** built from its key content.

> All materials are print-and-play. Most work best in pairs or small groups, but several can be played solo or as a whole class.

## The game formats (and why they work)

| Game | How it builds memory | Best for |
|------|----------------------|----------|
| **Loop cards (dominoes)** | Each card answers the previous and asks the next; the class/group must complete the whole loop — everyone stays engaged and errors surface instantly. | Whole class or small group; warm-up/plenary |
| **Taboo** | Describing a term *without* its obvious words forces deep understanding, not parroting. | Pairs/teams |
| **Bingo** | Hearing a definition and matching the term is rapid recognition recall under a little pressure. | Whole class |
| **Quick-fire "Guess the term"** | High-volume, low-stakes retrieval — the single most evidence-backed revision activity. | Pairs or solo |
| **Card sorts / "Apply it" / Top Trumps** (topic-specific) | Categorising, applying to scenarios, or comparing values deepens links between facts. | Small groups |

> 💡 **Why games, not just re-reading:** the act of *retrieving* an answer (even getting it wrong, then correcting it) strengthens memory far more than re-reading notes. Games make students retrieve again and again — exactly what the exam rewards.

## How to run a great revision-games session
1. **Revise first.** Students skim the [knowledge organiser](../knowledge-organisers/README.md) so the game is *recall*, not guessing.
2. **Print and cut** the cards (loop/Taboo/card-sort sections are laid out one item per block).
3. **Play, then reflect:** after the game, students RAG-rate the topic on their [tracker](../guides/self-assessment-tracker.md) and note anything they got wrong.
4. **Escalate:** follow a game with a [worksheet](../worksheets/README.md) or [mini-paper](../mini-papers/README.md) to convert the recall into written exam marks.

**Verified content:** numeric games (1.4 conversions, 2.3 Big O / Top Trumps) have had their answers checked, and the loop-card chains are written to close into a complete loop — so they're safe to print and play straight away.
