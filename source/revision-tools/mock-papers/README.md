# Full Synoptic Mock Papers

Topic mini-papers test one area at a time. **Mock papers** mimic the real thing: full-length, **synoptic** (questions drawn from across the whole component), and timed to the actual **2h 30m / 140 marks**. Sitting these builds the stamina, pacing and topic-switching that the real exams demand.

Each paper comes with a **complete worked mark scheme**, so you can mark yourself and re-RAG straight after.

## How to sit a mock properly

1. **Exam conditions.** One sitting, 2h 30m, no notes, no phone, cover the mark scheme.
2. **Pace it:** ~1 minute per mark. Flag and skip anything that stalls you; return at the end. Leave ~10 minutes to check.
3. **Show all working** on calculations, logic and traces — method marks are real marks.
4. **Plan the long answers** (9–12 markers) with a quick margin skeleton before writing.
5. **Mark honestly** against the scheme; total your marks and convert to a rough %.
6. **Diagnose:** log every dropped mark with its reason ([error log](../guides/common-mistakes.md)), and **re-RAG** weak sub-topics on your [tracker](../guides/self-assessment-tracker.md).
7. **Loop:** push weak areas back through the [organisers](../knowledge-organisers/README.md), [flashcards](../flashcards/README.md) and [mini-papers](../mini-papers/README.md), then re-attempt.

## Rough grade feel

Grade boundaries shift year to year, but as a working guide on a 140-mark paper: a secure **pass** sits roughly in the low half, and **A/A\*** candidates are reliably scoring in the upper range *with the AO2/AO3 (apply/evaluate/design) marks banked*, not just the recall. Use your mark to target revision — the **questions you got wrong** are your syllabus for next week.

Build up to these: master topics → mini-papers → these full mocks → official past papers. That progression is how a pass becomes an A\*.
